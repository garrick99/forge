A separate commercial license is required for:

- **Production deployment** of code compiled by Forge in commercial products or services

- **Offering Forge as a service** (hosted compilation, verification-as-a-service)
- **Distributing closed-source derivatives** of the Forge compiler

## What Counts as "Production"

If Forge-compiled code runs in a system that generates revenue, processes
customer data, or operates infrastructure for a commercial entity, that
is production use and requires a commercial license.

## What Does NOT Require a Commercial License

- Academic papers and research
- Open-source projects under OSI-approved licenses
- Internal evaluation and proof-of-concept work

## FAQ

**Q: I'm a student/researcher. Do I need a commercial license?**
A: No. Academic and research use is fully covered by Apache 2.0.

**Q: I want to use Forge in an open-source project.**
A: If your project is open-source under an OSI-approved license, Apache 2.0 applies. No commercial license needed.

**Q: My company wants to evaluate Forge.**
A: Evaluation and prototyping are covered by Apache 2.0. A commercial license is only needed when you deploy to production.

**Q: Can I contribute to Forge?**
A: Yes. Contributions are welcome under the Apache 2.0 license.
